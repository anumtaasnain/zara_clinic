NARRATIVE THERAPY

NARRATIVE THERAPY STEPS

Step 1

You and your therapist explore how the problem story controls your life and limits your possibilities. You identify the themes, characters, and plots that keep the story going. Think of it like taking apart a puzzle piece by piece, questioning its power and influence.

Step 2

You rediscover forgotten strengths, values, and experiences that contradict the problem story. Remember times you overcame challenges or felt like your authentic self. These glimpses become seeds for new, empowering narratives about who you are and what you can achieve.

Step 3

The problem story gets a name and becomes an external character, separate from you. You write or perform alternative stories, giving voice to your preferred selves and counter-narratives.


Narrative therapy Effects, and Process

Reduced self-blame: Shift from seeing yourself as the problem to seeing the problematic story as the issue. Increased agency: Reclaim control over your narrative and empower yourself to make positive changes. Boosted self-esteem: Discover and celebrate your strengths and counter-narratives. Improved relationships: Strengthen connections by building mutual understanding through shared stories. Process: Deconstruct: Explore the dominant story holding you back. Identify its themes, characters, and impact on your life. Excavate: Rediscover your strengths, values, and experiences that challenge the dominant story. Find seeds for counter-narratives. Rewrite: Externalize the problem story. Tell new stories that showcase your preferred selves and rewrite your future. Key points: It's collaborative, you're the co-author of your new story. Externalizing the problem creates space for positive change. Storytelling and creative approaches are encouraged.

What is narrative therapy?

Seeing problems as separate stories: You're not the problem, it's just a narrative holding you back. Discovering your hidden strengths: Reclaiming your voice and rediscovering forgotten chapters of your resilience. Rewriting your future: Creating new, empowering stories that guide you towards a brighter ending.
