Orthopedic Physiotherapy

Orthopedic Physiotherapy Steps

Medical History

Gathering information about the patient's medical history, including the onset of symptoms, any previous injuries or surgeries, and relevant medical conditions

Palpation

Using hands-on techniques to palpate and assess the soft tissues, joints, and bony structures for tenderness, swelling, or abnormalitie

Therapeutic Exercise

Prescribing specific exercises to address muscle imbalances, improve strength, enhance flexibility, and promote overall functional fitness.


Orthopedic Physiotherapy:Benefits, Side Effects, and Process

Orthopedic physiotherapy focuses on restoring functional abilities, helping individuals perform daily activities, work tasks, and recreational pursuits with greater ease. Providing individuals with a set of exercises to perform at home to reinforce progress achieved during in-clinic sessions. After engaging in therapeutic exercises, individuals may experience temporary muscle soreness, which is a normal response to increased activity.

What is Orthopedic Physiotherapy?

Orthopedic physiotherapy is a specialized branch of physiotherapy that focuses on the assessment, treatment, and rehabilitation of musculoskeletal conditions and injuries.Orthopedic physiotherapy plays a crucial role in helping individuals recover from injuries, manage chronic conditions, and improve their overall musculoskeletal well-being. The goal of orthopedic physiotherapy is to alleviate pain, improve mobility, restore function, and enhance overall musculoskeletal health.
