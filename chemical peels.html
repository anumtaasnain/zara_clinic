Chemical Peels: Benefits, Side Effects, and Process

Chemical peels are cosmetic procedures used to improve the appearance of the skin on the face, neck, or hands. They involve the application of a chemical solution to the skin, which causes it to exfoliate and eventually peel off. The benefits of chemical peels can vary depending on the type of peel and the specific skin concerns being addressed. The skin becomes more sensitive to sunlight after a chemical peel. It's essential to use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with high SPF to protect the skin from UV damage. Failure to do so may result in increased pigmentation and other complications. Some individuals may experience mild swelling, particularly with deeper peels. This typically subsides within a few days.

What is Chemical Peels?

A chemical peel is a cosmetic procedure that involves the application of a chemical solution to the skin, causing it to exfoliate and eventually peel off. The primary goal of a chemical peel is to improve the skin's appearance by addressing various skin concerns, such as fine lines, wrinkles, uneven pigmentation, and acne.
