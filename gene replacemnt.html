GENE REPLACEMENT THERAPY: Benefits, Side Effects, and Process

With gene therapy, healthcare providers deliver a healthy copy of a gene to cells inside your body. This healthy gene may replace a damaged (mutated) gene, inactivate a mutated gene or introduce an entirely new gene. Gene addition: Gene addition inserts a new copy of a gene into your cells. The body's immune system may recognize the introduced genetic material as foreign and mount an immune response, potentially causing inflammation and eliminating the modified cells. There are ongoing ethical debates about the use of gene therapy, especially in the context of germline editing, where changes are made to the genes that can be passed on to future generations.

What is gene therapy?

Gene therapy is a technique that modifies a person's genes to treat or cure disease. Gene therapies can work by several mechanisms: Replacing a disease-causing gene with a healthy copy of the gene. Inactivating a disease-causing gene that is not functioning properly.
