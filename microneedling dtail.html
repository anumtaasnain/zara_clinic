Microneedling

Microneedling Steps

Cleansing and Preparation

The initial step of microneedling involves thoroughly cleansing the skin to remove makeup and impurities, preparing it for the procedure.

Topical Anesthesia Application

The second step of microneedling involves applying a topical anesthetic to numb the skin, minimizing discomfort during the procedure.
Microneedling Procedure

The third step involves using a dermaroller or microneedling device to create controlled micro-injuries on the skin, stimulating collagen production and promoting skin rejuvenation.

Microneedling: Benefits, Side Effects, and Process

Microneedling offers numerous benefits, including improved skin texture, reduced fine lines and wrinkles, diminished scarring, even skin tone, and enhanced absorption of skincare products Common side effects of microneedling are temporary and include mild redness, slight swelling, and potential bruising. Microneedling involves cleansing the skin, applying a topical anesthetic for comfort, and using a dermaroller or microneedling device to create controlled micro-injuries.

What is Microneedling?

Microneedling is a cosmetic procedure that involves the use of a dermaroller or a microneedling device to create controlled micro-injuries on the skin's surface. The purpose of microneedling is to improve skin texture, reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, diminish scarring (including acne scars), address hyperpigmentation, and promote an overall rejuvenated and youthful complexion.
