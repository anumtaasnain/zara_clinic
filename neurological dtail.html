Neurological Physiotherapy

Neurological Physiotherapy Steps

Medical History

Gather information about the patient's medical history, including the onset and progression of neurological symptoms, any relevant medical diagnoses, surgeries, or medications

Neurological Examination

Perform a thorough neurological examination to assess muscle strength, tone, coordination, reflexes, sensation, and other neurological functions.

Development of Treatment Goals

Once the assessment is complete, the physiotherapist can develop an individualized treatment plan that addresses the specific needs and goals of the patient


Neurological Physiotherapy:Benefits, Side Effects, and Process

Treatment in neurological physiotherapy often includes a combination of exercises, functional activities, balance training, gait retraining, and other therapeutic interventions to improve overall mobility and functional independence. Regular reassessment is conducted to monitor progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.

What is neurological therapy?

Neurological physiotherapy is a specialized branch of physiotherapy that focuses on the assessment and treatment of individuals with neurological conditions affecting the central nervous system (CNS) and peripheral nervous system. The central nervous system includes the brain and spinal cord, while the peripheral nervous system involves the nerves outside the brain and spinal cord. The goal of neurological physiotherapy is to help individuals with these conditions maximize their physical function, mobility, and independence.
