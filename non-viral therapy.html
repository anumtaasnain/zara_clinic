NON-VIRAL GENE THERAPY STEPS

Step 1

The first is non-viral gene therapy is the delivery of the therapeutic gene to target cells, can be achieved using microinjection, electroporation, gene gun, ultrasound-mediated methods

Step 2

The therapeutic gene needs to enter the cell nucleus to exert its therapeutic effect can be achieved nuclear localization signals

Step 3

The final step in non-viral gene therapy is the therapeutic effect can be achieved using RNA interference, gene editing, and gene replacement


NON-VIRAL GENE THERAPY benefits,side effects, and process

Genetic therapies hold promise to treat many diseases, but they are still new approaches to treatment and may have risks. Potential risks could include certain types of cancer, allergic reactions, or damage to organs or tissues if an injection is involved. Non-viral methods allow for the delivery of larger genes and can accommodate different types of genetic material, providing more flexibility in the types of genes that can be delivered.

What is Non-Viral Gene therapy?

Physical methods allow researchers to directly deliver genetic material to target cells (for example, like using an injection needle). Chemical methods use natural or synthetic materials that are compatible with the human body, so they are not as likely to generate immune responses.
