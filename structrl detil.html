STRUCTURAL FAMILY THERAPY

STRUCTURAL FAMILY THERAPY

Joining

The therapist joins the family, building trust and observing interactions. They gather information about family history, roles, and communication patterns. This creates a "map" of the family dynamics, identifying power structures, boundaries, and communication flows.

Step 2

The therapist helps the family shift from blaming individuals to understanding how the system itself contributes to challenges.

Step 3

The therapist provides tools and exercises for the family to practice the restructured system in daily life. This could involve role-playing scenarios, practicing active listening, or holding regular family meetings.
